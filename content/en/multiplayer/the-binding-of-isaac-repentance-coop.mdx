<Section icon={Settings} title="Core Mechanics and Resource Management">

In **the binding of isaac repentance coop**, the game's difficulty scales dynamically based on the number of players. Bosses gain additional health, and room layouts may become more crowded to compensate for the increased fire power of a four-man team. The most significant change, however, is how resources are handled. Unlike solo play where Isaac hoards everything, co-op requires a democratic approach to loot.

Resources are categorized into two types: shared and individual. Managing these correctly is often the difference between a Dead God run and a quick trip back to the main menu.

| Resource Type | Shared or Individual | Impact on Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coins** | **Shared** | Group must decide on Shop purchases together. |
| **Bombs** | **Shared** | Use sparingly for Secret Rooms and Tinted Rocks. |
| **Keys** | **Shared** | Prioritize Treasure Rooms over locked chests. |
| **Items (Pedestals)** | **Individual** | Picked up by one player; benefits that character only. |
| **Health (Hearts)** | **Individual** | Players manage their own red/soul/black hearts. |
| **Trinkets/Cards/Pills** | **Individual** | Held by one player; can be dropped for others. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Depletion Warning">
Because keys and bombs are shared, a four-player team can run out of resources four times faster. Always check if a teammate needs a specific pickup before using the last bomb on a random rock.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Setting Up Repentance+ Online Coop">

The introduction of the Repentance+ DLC brought native online support to Steam, removing the need for third-party tools like Remote Play Together. Setting up a session is straightforward but requires all participants to have their game versions synchronized. Follow these steps to start your first online run.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="DLC Verification">
    Ensure all players own Rebirth, Afterbirth, Afterbirth+, and Repentance. Then, download the free **Repentance+** DLC from the Steam store page.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Lobby Creation">
    From the main menu, select 'Online Play'. Choose 'Host' to create a private lobby or 'Quick Match' to join random players.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Inviting Friends">
    In the lobby screen, use the 'Invite' button to open your Steam Friends list. Once your friends join, you will see their avatars in the lobby.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Character Selection">
    Each player selects their character. Note that players can only pick characters they have already unlocked on their own save file.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Starting the Descent">
    The host chooses the difficulty (Normal, Hard, or Greedier). Once everyone is 'Ready', the host starts the run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Online Latency">
Online co-op is peer-to-peer. For the best experience, the player with the most stable internet connection should act as the host to minimize input lag for others.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Progression, Unlocks, and Death Mechanics">

One of the greatest benefits of **the binding of isaac repentance coop** is the shared progression system. When a boss is defeated, the completion mark is added to the post-it note of every character present in the run. This allows players to help each other finish difficult characters like Tainted Lost or Jacob and Esau by providing a "safety net" of extra players.

However, death works differently in multiplayer. If a player dies, they don't immediately end the run. Instead, they turn into a ghost with limited interaction.
